Shawn Michaels | Career & Records

Shawn Michaels was born on July 22, 1965, in Chandler, Arizona, the United States, to Richard Hickenbottom and Carol Hickenbottom. 

With guidance and training from Jose Lothario, Michaels made his professional wrestling debut in 1984 with the National Wrestling Alliance. 

In 1987, he signed a contract with the World Wrestling Federation but got fired due to a misunderstanding. However, one year later, he was rehired by the Company and debuted at a live event in 1988.

Later, in 1993 Shawn Michaels partnered with Diesel and was awarded by Dave Meltzer for his match with Razor Ramon at WrestleMania X five-stars.

Shawn Michaels’ first run with WWE lasted from 1988 to 1998, a testament to the greatness of ‘The Boy Toy.’

Some of the famous opponents Michaels wrestled with were Kurt Angle, John Cena, and Edge. In 2010, he had his last wrestling with The Undertaker at WrestleMania 25. 

Shawn Michaels’s Final Match 

In January 2010, Shawn Michaels buried the hatchet with Bret Hart, his long-time rival. Both of the competitors shook hands and hugged in the ring.

Likewise, in WrestleMania XXVI, The Undertaker had victory over him, resulting in Michaels being forced to retire due to the march stipulation. 

On the March 29 episode of Raw, Michaels gave an emotional farewell speech ending with his familiar sentence, “Shawn Michaels has left the building.”
