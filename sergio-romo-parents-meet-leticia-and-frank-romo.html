Sergio Romo Parents: Meet Leticia And Frank Romo: Sergio Romo, officially known as Sergio Francisco Romo is an American former professional baseball pitcher born on March 4, 1983.

He developed a love for baseball at a younger age and was consistent throughout his career rising through the ranks to become one of the most sought-after baseball players.

Sergio Romo played in MLB for the San Francisco Giants, Los Angeles Dodgers, Tampa Bay Rays, Miami Marlins, Minnesota Twins, Oakland Athletics, Seattle Mariners, and Toronto Blue Jays.

Growing up, he attended four colleges and was drafted in the 28th round of the 2005 draft by the Giants where reached the major leagues in 2008 and appeared in 45 games for the Giants in 2009.

In 2010, Sergio Romo became the setup man for Giants closer Brian Wilson as the Giants won the 2010 World Series

He posted a 1.50 earned run average (ERA) in 2011, then became the closer for the Giants in 2012, recording three saves during the 2012 World Series as the Giants won the title.

Sergio Romo was an All-Star in 2013, and though he lost the closer role the next year, he won his third World Series ring as the Giants defeated the Kansas City Royals in the 2014 World Series.

After two more seasons with the Giants, Romo signed with the Dodgers in 2017. He was designated for assignment in 2018 and traded to the Rays.

In 2018, he was the first pitcher used by Rays manager Kevin Cash as part of Tampa’s opener strategy. Later that year, he became Tampa Bay’s closer.

A free agent after the season, he signed with the Marlins and served as Miami’s closer until getting traded to the Twins halfway through the year.

Sergio Romo reached the playoffs with Minnesota in 2019 and 2020 before becoming a free agent once again.
