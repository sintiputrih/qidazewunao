Early Life and Career Beginnings

Gladys Knight was born on May 28, 1944, in Atlanta, Georgia. She began singing in the church choir at the age of four and won a televised talent hunt at the age of seven. In 1952, she formed “The Pips” with her siblings and cousins, which later became known as “Gladys Knight & The Pips.”

Rise to Stardom

Solo Career

In 1987, Knight embarked on a solo career and released her third solo album, “Good Woman,” in 1991. The album was a commercial success, reaching No. 1 on the R&B album chart. She continued to release successful albums and singles throughout the 1990s and 2000s.

Authorship

Awards and Accolades

Over her illustrious career, Knight has received numerous awards and accolades. She is a seven-time Grammy Award winner and has been inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame. In 2007, she received the BET Lifetime Achievement Award.
