Kamila Valieva- Professional Career

Valieva began her skating career in 2009 at RSDUSSHOR in Kazan. Her first coach was Ksenia Ivanova, who was followed by Marina Kudriavtseva, Igor Lyutikov, and Natalia Dubinskaya. Valieva now holds the women’s short program, free skating, and total score world records. She has set nine world records in her career. She is the first female skater to exceed the 250-, 260-, and 270-point total score standards in a single season, as well as the 170- and 180-point free skate thresholds and the 90-point short program criterion.

Valieva is the second woman, after training partner and teammate Alexandra Trusova, to land a quadruple toe loop, the fourth woman to land any type of quadruple jump, the 16th woman to land a triple Axel, and the third woman, after Alysa Liu and training partner and teammate Sofia Akateva, to land a triple Axel and quadruple jump in the same program. Kamila is the 2020 World Junior champion, the winner of the 2019–20 Junior Grand Prix Final, and the Russian junior national champion in 2020. Valieva is also the Russian National 2021 silver medalist.
