How Did Ore Oduba Pursue His Career? Professional Life

Oduba joined The Network, an Edinburgh television festival scheme, because he wanted to pursue a career in sports broadcasting. Oduba joined Newsround in August 2008, after Lizo Mzimba and Laura Jones had left. His first role on the show was as a reporter and presenter of the breakfast bulletin. In 2009, he was quickly promoted to deputy presenter, filling in for the main presenter Sonali Shah when she was unavailable.

In 2010, he joined Shah in presenting the main Newsround bulletin twice. This was the case until Shah’s departure in November 2011. Reporters Leah Gooding and Hayley Cutts later joined him. He also hosted the spin-off show Sportsround as part of his role. When the show was relaunched in October 2008, he took over as the primary host. He performed until the show was canceled in 2010.

Oduba was a regular performer on CBBC studio presentations after his Newsround shift. He appeared on CBBC shows such as Sam and Mark’s TMi Friday, Hacker Time, and All Over the Place. Oduba hosted a CBBC program called Ultimate Sports Day in the run-up to the 2012 Summer Olympics. In addition, he co-hosts live cinema relays from the Royal Opera House in Covent Garden with former ballerina Darcey Bussell.

He took part in a Sport Relief challenge called “Hell on High Seas” on March 7, 2016, in which he sailed 1,000 miles from Belfast to London with five other celebrities, including Alex Jones and Suzi Perry. He co-hosted Sport Relief: Clash of the Titans with Dan Walker in 2016. Throughout August 2016, he provided coverage of the 2016 Summer Olympics on BBC Four.
