Mike Vrabel Wife, Parents, Family, Height, Net Worth, Bio

August 2024 · 4 minute read

Mike Vrabel is a former National Football League player who played for the Pittsburgh Steelers, New England Patriots and Kansas City Chiefs before choosing to become a coach, while he earns a living working as a coach- leader of the Tennessee Titans.

The three-time Super Bowl-winning linebacker who became an All-Pro in 2007, the same year he made his first and only Pro-Bowl appearance, he was known for his versatility as a player, as he usually played the role of a tight opponent. The NFL Network notably named him number 7 on the list of the 10 most versatile players.

Biography,

Mike Vrabel was born Michael George Vrabel in Akron, Ohio on August 14, 1975. He spent most of his life in the American Midwest. He first attended Jordanian Walsh High School in Cuyahoga Falls, before heading to Ohio State University for an athletic scholarship after a successful high school career..

Vrabel played as a defensive end for the Ohio Buckeyes football team member from 1993 to 1996. During that time, he was twice named the Big Ten Defensive Lineman of the Year ( 1995 and 1996) and was recognized as a consensus First Team All-American team. He recorded a total of 36 sacks and 66 tackles for a loss in his college career before enlisting in the 1997 NFL Draft, where he was selected with the 91st pick in the third round by the Pittsburgh Steelers. .

The Steelers benefited from the services of Vrabel for four seasons before joining the New England Patriots as a free agent for the 2001-2002 season. During his eight years with the Patriots, Vrabel won three Super Bowls and was a First Team All-Pro selection as well as the Pro Bowl in 2007. On February 27, 2009, Mike Vrabel’s time with the Patriots s ended was traded to Kansas City Chiefs. He then played two more grueling seasons in the NFL before choosing to end his career. He wasn’t done with the game as a whole, however, as he almost immediately took a job coaching linebackers at Ohio State, where he was inducted into the Athletics Hall of Fame.

Vrabel spent three years in Ohio before being hired by the Houston Texans as a linebackers coach in 2014. He remained there for three years before being promoted to defensive coordinator. After a year in his new role, Vrabel left the Texans in January 2018 to work as head coach of the Tennessee Titans.
