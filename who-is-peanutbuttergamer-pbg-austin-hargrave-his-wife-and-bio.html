Who is PeanutButterGamer, PBG, (Austin Hargrave)?His wife and bio

June 2024 · 4 minute read

PeanutButterGamer (PBG) is an American YouTuber gamer and the star of The Peanut Butter Show. He’s known for his game reviews, Top 10 lists, and many other features, including Kill an Avatar (exploring the cruelty potential of video gaming) and The G-Files (looking at weird things in video games.

PBG is the co-founder of NormalBoots and owns a game channel he called PBGGameplay with several highly edited series of Let’s Play. He was one of the contributors to Blistered Thumbs, a subdivision of Channel Awesome, where video games are usually the main topic of discussion.

Biography of PeanutButterGamer

PeanutButterGamer, real name Austin Hargrave, was born on July 25, 1990 in Dallas, Texas. He spent his early years in Texas before settling in Seattle, Washington.

As a kid, he always loved games and started making gaming videos at a very young age of 12. As he continued to post some of the videos he had made with his friends on various YouTube channels, Hargrave eventually decided to open his own channel, devoted to game reviews and top 10. He was 19 at the time. he era and his tireless efforts turned the YouTube channel “PeanutButterGamer” into one of the most famous gaming media.

Career

PBG uploaded his first YouTube video, Top 10 Weirdest and Scariest Video Game Characters, in October 2009. His journey to creating videos and reviews began in 2010, while working for ScrewAttack in as a member of the community. As he gained popularity, the mainstream was enough for him to quit his job and start creating YouTube videos full time.

Over the years, PBG has gained huge followers ranging in the thousands and millions. PeanutButterGamer, its main channel, has over 1.9 million subscribers, while its gaming channel, PBGGameplay, has gained over 850,000 subscribers. PBG has also garnered over 700,000 views on Twitch Tv, where he posts videos such as Animal Crossing and The Elder Scroll Oblivion.

He posted several other videos, including the popular Hardcore series and the Collection series where every item is put together in a set of various games. PeanutButterGamer’s videos are mostly made up of humorous montages with very random effects and text. The second channel he created, PBGGameplay focuses on Zelda and Minecraft Hardcore collection series.

Other popular videos include Diablo Game Adventures. The Legend of Zelda is one of his favorite video games. It featured the month of Zelda in 2011, around the same time (November) the new Zelda game Sky Sword was slated for release. It was during the same period that he added the information relating to Zelda. gameplay, reviews, and Top 10 to his channel.

Although he plays variety games, PBG is rarely harsh when criticizing the games he reviews. Prior to his massive success in game reviews, he originally presented skits surrounding games, but then moved on to Top 10 and series like To Kill An Avatar, which was themed around the death of innocent creatures. in games; and The G Files which was a show about weird things in video games.

However, he dropped the shows saying he didn’t represent the message he wanted to convey with his content, but he kept the top 10 lists before starting the game reviews. It therefore gained a reputation for its good quality editing and haphazard humor. PBG has a third channel which he said is created as the “same channel”.

Besides their personal channels, PBG created Normal Boots (2010) in collaboration with Jonathan Jafari, “JonTron”, to act as a hub where they can post content and receive ad revenue.

Although it was shut down in 2012, Normal Boot was relaunched on January 24, 2014, with the inclusion of other video game-focused channels, ProJared, “Satchbag Goods”, and The Completionist.

PBG periodically engages in video piracy, where he plays in games like Super Mario 64 using cheat codes and glitches, which has also become one of the most popular videos on his channel.

As for when he usually uploads videos, there are no specific times or days, but he usually posts every three weeks on his main channel and several times a week on his gaming channel.

His wife

PeanutButterGamer and his wife Danielle “Unicorism” Hargrave, an artist, married in 2015 and currently live in Woodinville, Washington with their dog Luka. Little information has been released about his wife, she seems to have gained popularity due to her marriage to the YouTube star.
