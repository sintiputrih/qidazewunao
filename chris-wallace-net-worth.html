Chris Wallace Net Worth

August 2024 · 3 minute read

Chris Wallace’s net worth is an estimated $6 million. He was born on 12th October 1947 in Chicago, Illinois, USA, and comes from a Jewish background. Chris Wallace is a well-known journalist and anchor, recognized for hosting the program “Fox News Sunday”. His tenure with Fox News Channel of more than a decade has resulted in numerous awards, contributing significantly to his current net worth.

Reportedly, Chris Wallace earns around $1 million annually as part of his role at Fox News and also contributes to the channel’s other shows, helping to solidify and increase his wealth.

Chris has been involved in journalism from an early age, having grown up around political journalism due to his father, reporter Mike Wallace, who is best known for his work on “60 Minutes”. Chris attended The Hotchkiss School and later went to Harvard College, where he began his reporting career at the school’s radio station. Despite being accepted at Yale Law School, Chris pursued his passion for television journalism, taking a job with The Boston Globe.

After starting his career at NBC in 1975, Chris spent 14 years with the network, working as a political correspondent for “NBC News” and co-anchoring the “Today” show. He also fulfilled the roles of a moderator for “Meet the Press” and anchor for NBC “Nightly News” during his time there. In 1989, he joined ABC as a senior correspondent for “Primetime Thursday” and at times hosted the show “Nightline”. Notably, he reported on the Gulf War from Tel Aviv during his tenure with ABC. Subsequently, after working with ABC for 14 years, Chris made the move to Fox News Channel. Since 2003, he has been the host of “Fox News Sunday” and has also contributed as a guest on other Fox series, alongside making occasional appearances on Boston’s WRKO.

For his outstanding journalism work, Chris Wallace received the Paul White Award in 2013 from the Radio Television Digital News Association. He has also been honored with three Emmy Awards and a Dupont-Columbia Silver Baton Award.

In his personal life, Chris was first married to Elizabeth Farell in 1973 and they had four children before eventually divorcing. He later married Lorraine Smothers in 1997, who was previously married to comedian Dick Smothers. Lorraine has two children from her previous marriage. Chris is known for his active involvement in public service, being a registered Democrat and holding a position on the Jefferson Awards for Public Service Board of Selectors.

While these are the notable aspects of Chris Wallace’s life and career, his net worth and achievements stand as a testament to his successful journey in journalism, portraying his dedication and passion for the field.
